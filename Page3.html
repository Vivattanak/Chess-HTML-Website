Abdusattorov Wins Prague Masters With Round To Spare As New World Number 4

The Prague Masters has become a dream tournament for GM Nodirbek Abdusattorov. The PRAGUE INTERNATIONAL CHESS FESTIVAL is a 9 days tournament starting from 27. February - 7. March 2024. It is a highly prestigious and competitive tournament, located in the capital of the Czech republic. For more information about the tournament please visit their website .

In a field with three candidates, the 19-year-old Uzbek GM secured victory with a round to spare today while moving to world number four in the live ratings, surpassing World Champion Ding Liren.

Abdusattorov today defeated GM Parham Maghsoodloo in another tremendous fighting game not unlike their previous clash in Wijk aan Zee this year. The other winner in the round was GM Gukesh Dommaraju, who refuted dubious early middlegame play by GM Vincent Keymer.

The final round starts four hours earlier than usual, on Thursday at 5 a.m. ET / 11:00 CET / 3:30 p.m. IST. With 6/8, Abdusattorov is one and a half points ahead of the trio of Nguyen, Maghsoodloo, and Praggnanandhaa going into the final round.

The pairings for the final round are Praggnanandhaa-Navara, Keymer-Bartel, Maghsoodloo-Gukesh, Rapport-Abdusattorov, and Nguyen-Vidit.

The time control is 90 minutes for the first 40 moves, followed by 30 more minutes for the rest of the game, with a 30-second increment per move starting on move one.
